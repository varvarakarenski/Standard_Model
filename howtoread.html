How to Read the Standard Model

The mass of a particle is important because it describes how it interacts with the universe. It determines its behavior, including its resistance to acceleration (inertia), its gravitational interaction, and its energy.

The charge of a particle is important because it determines how the particle interacts with other charged particles and electromagnetic fields.

Particle spin is not an external rotation. It's a form of angular momentum, meaning it has both magnitude and direction, and it's quantized, meaning only certain values are allowed.
